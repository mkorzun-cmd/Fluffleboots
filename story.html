The Complete Poem

FLUFFLEBOOTS

by Melissa Nethken

Why aren't dogs called FLUFFLEBOOTS?
Why aren't cats called KAZOOS?
Who picked out those boring names?
It wasn't me. Did you?

Why is blue called blue at all?
I'd like to call it WHUMM.
And rain, I'll call it TIPPITY TAP
Thunder I'll call GA BRUM.

Someone made these words up once
and everyone agreed.
They shook on it and wrote it down
and called it A-B-C.

But here's the thing about a word —
It's just a sound we chose.
Nobody asked the elephant
what to call her nose.

🐝

Nobody asked the bumblebee
what she calls her buzz.
Nobody asked the grizzly bear
what he calls his fuzz.

Nobody asked the belly button.
Nobody asked the knee.
Nobody asked them for their names —
not anyone. Not me.

I've got some words all of my own,
they live behind my eyes.
ZIBBLEFRIZZ and BOOFNSNOOT.
Each one a small surprise.

I think I'll name the clouds today.
I think I'll name the sheep.
I'll pick a word that names the way
I feel when I'm asleep.

If I can name a raindrop
Then you can name a star.
So what's YOUR word for thunder?
Shout it loud and far!

The whole wide world is waiting
for names from someone new.
Maybe someone is FLUFFLEBOOTS.
Maybe someone is you.

For Publishing Inquiries

FLUFFLEBOOTS is a completed picture book manuscript for ages 3–4, the first in a planned series of books that hide a hard philosophical question inside a small, delightful one.
